    Has anyone found the recording created March 18, 2023 in Santiago, Chile of AURORA's Lollapalooza 2023 performance? The sound, late sun lighting, videography, and performances by all were extraordinary.

    It was a Youtube livestream, however the conversion of the live video to the stream was botched. I have the original Youtube livestream capture and it appears that the native video streamed to Youtube was 1080i/25 at a reasonable bitrate. However, Youtube does not take interlaced video.

    It should have been deinterlaced (QTGMC or Pro equivalent) to 1080p/25 or 1080p/50 before being compressed to progressive. It was not. They fed an interlaced 1080i/25 to Youtube's VP9 compressor that was set to progressive at 1080p/30... needless to say, it looks like crap. Interlacing is baked into the progressive frames.

    There is noticeable judder from the framerate mismatch. Looking at the stream with Videoredo clearly shows the nature of the damage- blobby blurred interlaced patterns appear in areas of motion. I don't know of any video processing that could fix this (not even Santiag for Avisynth). Topaz claims a new Dione AI model has a mode called Interlaced Progressive specifically made to fix videos suffering this damage. From what I've read, it doesn't work well (yet).

    Additionally, there are multiple gaps in the audio and/or video, as if it was live streamed from the venue without adequate buffering. All posted captures suffer from the exact same gaps (no need to post or link them).

    I'm hoping AURORA's label has realized just how great this recording turned out and has obtained a perfect high quality copy from the on-site Production team. I'm hoping they are streaming it somewhere for free or as Video-On-Demand (VOD). Please post the link if you find one. If you are skilled at stream capture, please post the capture.
