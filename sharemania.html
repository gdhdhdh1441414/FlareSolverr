    "K-POP" is a song by American rapper Travis Scott, Puerto Rican rapper Bad Bunny and Canadian singer the Weeknd, released on July 21, 2023, through Epic and Cactus Jack Records as the lead single from Scott's upcoming fourth studio album Utopia. Contrary to its title, the song isn't about the popular Korean music genre; rather, it delves into the concept of a lollipop containing the drug "Ketamine". The three artists wrote the song with producers Illangelo, BNYX of Working on Dying, Boi-1da, and Jahaan Sweet.

    Scott announced the collaboration on his social media accounts on July 19, 2023. Both Bad Bunny and the Weeknd had previously been among artists named to appear on Utopia, with Scott previewing an unreleased song featuring Bad Bunny at a club in Monaco in May 2023. Bad Bunny said in an interview with Rolling Stone in its July/August 2023 issue that he and Scott "worked on that a while back".

    An accompanying music video for "K-pop" was released on July 21, 2023. The video features Scott "getting his groove on" in an empty football stadium that has been covered with Utopia branding. He teases a potential stadium tour in support of Utopia with signage that reads "Stadium Tour Rehearsal", while sharing a look at his upcoming sneaker collaboration with Air Jordan. Bad Bunny is seen making his way through a nightclub, and the Weeknd is spotted enjoying a meal with Scott in a "unique" mansion surrounded by women. The visual features cameo appearances from Matthew Williams, Pharrell Williams, and SZA.
