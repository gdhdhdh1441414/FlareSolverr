    "Olé" is a song by English singer John Newman. The song was released as a digital download on 8 July 2016 as a non-album single. This stripped-back slice of reggae was written and produced by John Newman's friend and regular creative partner Scottish DJ and record producer Calvin Harris. The pair also teamed up on Harris' 2014 hit single "Blame."

    The song was written from the perspective of a guy with a secret lover who is cheating on her partner. The lyrics prompted speculation that they are about Harris' breakup with Taylor Swift and the songstress' immediate hookup with the British actor Tom Hiddleston - the implication is that Swift had started seeing Hiddleston before she split with Harris. The American songstress is known for writing songs about her failed romances, but this song's release immediately provoked internet chatter that the Scot had pipped her to the post with this cut.

    Sources close to Harris denied the track is about the Scottish DJ's breakup with Swift, telling US Weekly that the lyrics were written months before they split. Regarding Harris' inspiration for the lyrics, Newman said: "In any situation, Calvin could tell me a backstory, but it wouldn't matter because I'm the one who's got to sing it. But look we've all fallen in love with somebody's girlfriend or whatever. We've all gotten into messy relationship situations and that's how I related to it by having done that in the past."

    The song's opening line, "Low key, you won't tell none of your friends about me," potentially points to Hiddleston, who is well known for his villainous character Loki in The Avengers.
