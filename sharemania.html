    "The Time Machine" is a instrumental track by English rock musician Alan Parsons. It's the title track of his third solo album, released in 1999. While the sound of this album is similar to some of the soft, ethereal tracks by the Alan Parsons Project, none of the writing or performance credits in the sleeve notes go to Alan Parsons, except for one short and simple instrumental part on "Temporalia". The other instrumentals were written by drummer Stuart Elliott and guitarist Ian Bairnson). Parsons relation to the album is almost exclusively as producer.

    The video promo of "The Time Machine" was made by Dutch dance music DJ, remixer and producer Ben Liebrand and was released on 1 September 1999. It includes 2 designs by Storm Thorgerson (Hipgnosis UK). One design by Storm was 'the crazy tube city'. Another one is the man dragging the huge watch over the beach. These designs which were pencil drawings, were then translated into 3D models and used in the clip.
    Everything was modeled, animated and rendered by Ben Liebrand in approx 4 weeks time. The modeling and animation was done in Softimage 3D Extreme. The rendering was done on 8 Silicon Graphics R10000 processors devided over 2 Octane's and 2 Origin 200 servers.

    Ben Liebrand wrote about the clip:
    "I was fortunate enough to be chosen to do the video promo. I designed the Time Machine in a Jules Verne style, depicted the "Science Fiction Look" of the year 1900. Other scenes in the video clip like the "Tube City" and the "Man Dragging The Clock" are based on artwork created by Storm Thorgusson, famous for his Pink Floyd cover artwork. The airplane at the end was based on an actual Jules Verne drawing.
    Time for this project was 4 weeks, in which to model, render and edit the complete video. Every time a model or scene took shape, I posted a picture for Alan Parsons and Storm Thorgusson to approve. These same pictures and the page I made then can be seen here: http://liebrand.com/prj/aptm/
    A fun part was to have sun-dials show the time arranged in way to form the numbers in a digital read out style. I chose the numbers 12:45 as that would hopefully be recognised by most as indicating time. Just days before the single and clip would be released, the character "Austin Powers" happened to mention "The Alan Parsons Project" in his new movie. The sound bites were added to the single and clip although there was no artistic need or reason to do so other than benefitting from the films media exposure. This version however shows the clip as it was originally intended."
