    "1999" is a remix by EDM duo Binary Finary. Binary Finary are a British trance act, originally comprising Matt Laws, Ricky Grant and Stuart Matheson. They first gained widespread recognition in 1997 with the release of their hit single "1998." This track made a significant impact on the club scene and became the first electronic dance track to enter the UK Top 30. It took dance floors by storm, with its powerful chord changes, heart-stopping breakdown and dramatic crescendos. The track has been featured on numerous mix compilations and has proved a favourite with many notable DJs such as Paul Van Dyk (who remixed the track), Paul Oakenfold and Judge Jules, solidifying its status as a Trance Classic. Positiva Records re-released the track as ‘1999’ in that year, in a two-disc format, featuring remixes from Van Dyk, Matt Darey, Gouryella (DJ Tiësto and Ferry Corsten) and Kaycee. It proved a crossover success, reaching number 11 in the UK charts. Binary Finary performed live at numerous club events and festivals in 1998 and 1999, including Glastonbury Festival, Cream’s Creamfields and in Ibiza.

    Now, 25 years later, Binary Finary continue to captivate fans and critics around the world with their innovative music and live performances. Their live shows are known for their technical prowess and immersive experience, as the duo expertly combines visuals, lighting, and live mixing to create a memorable experience for their audience.

    In addition to their original productions, Binary Finary has also built a reputation for their remixes, giving new life to classic tracks while introducing them to a new generation of dance music fans. This ability to continuously evolve and stay relevant in the ever-changing world of electronic dance music has been a key factor in their longevity and success.
